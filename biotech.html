Biotech

In less than a decade, the biotechnology field has matured from a frontier science into a well-established industry. The industry has witnessed significant advances in human health, agricultural production and biodefense. Still, it presents thrilling opportunities for the inventor as new technologies are developed, tested, and eventually produced for broad application. However, the excitement inherent to this industry is tempered by its intrinsic risks: the high costs of research and development; the long lead time required to develop new products; and potential legal problems for biotechnology firms, including regulatory issues, intellectual property protection and the potential for litigation. We assist our biotechnology clients on matters, including all manner of intellectual property protection licensing, technology transfer and franchising, and public policy in different countries.
